6 Surprising Signs of Pregnancy You Probably Had No Idea About

Posted by Martina Birk on Saturday, August 3, 2024

Although pregnancy is without a doubt a wonderful time, it often comes with pretty weird and unpleasant symptoms. And some of them might appear very early, even before your pregnancy test comes out positive. From an unusual taste in your mouth to dizziness, there are lots of annoying signs of pregnancy you surely will forget about after you have your baby.

1. Your toilet seat might turn blue.

If you’ve been noticing blue stains on your toilet seat you probably think your jeans are leaving some of their blue dye on your skin. But this phenomenon might actually be a sign of pregnancy. Although there are no scientific explanations for it, many moms-to-be notice that their toilet seats are turning blue. One of the reasons might be an antibacterial coating on your toilet seat that reacts with your pregnancy hormones. Your prenatal vitamin may also cause hormonal changes in your body, which in turn can affect the color of your toilet seat.

2. You’re tasting metal.

Pregnant women can have very strange food cravings, but if you feel the metal taste in your mouth, there’s actually a scientific reason behind it. Pregnancy hormones affect your body in many different ways, and increases in estrogen and progesterone might cause you to have a metallic taste in your mouth. Luckily, you can easily get rid of it by chewing a gum or having some saltines.

3. Your gums get swollen.

If you start noticing that your gums are more sensitive than ever chances are you might be pregnant. Pregnancy affects blood flow in your body, causing your gums to swell and bleed. Make sure to floss at least once a day and use a toothbrush with soft bristles to alleviate the symptoms.

4. You have heartburn.

An annoying burning sensation in the center of your chest can appear after you had an overly spicy meal, but it might also be a sign of pregnancy. In fact, indigestion is very common during pregnancy because the growing baby keeps pressing against your stomach. To relieve this unpleasant sensation, try cutting down on caffeine and eating small meals often rather than larger meals 3 times a day.

5. You’re feeling bloated.

Bloating might be one of the earliest signs of pregnancy. When you’re expecting, your progesterone levels skyrocket, causing you to feel puffy even at the very beginning of your pregnancy. Progesterone slows down your digestion, allowing your baby to get the most nutrients from food and making you feel bloated.

6. You’re feeling dizzy.

The dizziness you may be feeling when getting out of bed might be one of the earliest signs of pregnancy. Increased blood flow may affect your blood pressure, causing you to feel lightheaded. Although feeling like the room is spinning each time you move surely isn’t pleasant, the good news is it’s not a cause for concern.

Which of these pregnancy signs surprised you the most? Have you experienced any of them?
