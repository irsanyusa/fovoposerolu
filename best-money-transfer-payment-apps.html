Here’s a look at our top picks for the best apps to receive and send money in the U.S. and across the world, and why you might choose each one.

PayPal is the first—and one of the most well-known—names in online payments, with more than 435 million active consumer and merchant accounts. This platform allows for protected transactions between businesses and customers, as well as the ability to send money between individuals. To date, PayPal has helped facilitate over $1.36 trillion in payment transactions across more than 200 markets worldwide. 

With PayPal, you can send money from almost anywhere in the world using your bank account, debit or credit card, or an existing PayPal account balance. You can also make purchases with built-in consumer protections, at no additional cost to you.

There is no fee to send money to friends and family when you use your bank account, PayPal balance, or Amex Send account—nor do you pay extra for goods and services covered by Purchase Protection. If you use a debit or credit card to send money to friends and family, you’ll be charged 2.9% plus $0.30 per transaction.

Businesses or individuals using PayPal to receive funds for goods and services will pay a fee per transaction plus a percentage of the funds received, depending on the transaction type and amount. Both consumers and merchants are covered by Purchase Protection for these eligible transactions, with PayPal offering dispute resolution and reimbursement if a covered issue arises.

There are many money transfer apps and platforms that make it easy to send funds from one bank account to another. But what if you want to send money to someone who doesn’t have a bank account, or you need to find a way to safely send cash either domestically or internationally?

Western Union has been around since 1851 and began offering money transfers as early as 1871. While you could originally send funds only between New York, Chicago, and Boston, Western Union now provides money transfer services in more than 200 countries.

With the Western Union, you can use your bank account, credit card, or debit card to send money to others, or you can deposit cash at an in-store location. The recipient can then receive their funds on the other end in a few different ways, including picking up cash at an agent location near them.

The fees for sending funds through Western Union vary depending on where you’re sending money from, where the recipient is located, how you’re funding the transfer, how much you’re sending, and how the recipient will receive the funds. It takes anywhere from a few minutes to multiple business days before the funds are available.

Released in 2009, Venmo is a popular subsidiary of the fintech giant, PayPal. Venmo was originally touted as a great way to send funds to friends, especially when it came to things like splitting the dinner bill or sharing expenses with roommates. Venmo’s mobile app has since gained around 90 million users, or about a third of U.S. adults. It quickly became one of the most popular payment apps in the world.

With Venmo, users can connect a savings account, checking account, or even a credit card for funding purposes. They can use these connected accounts to send money to friends, split bills, send gifts, or pay businesses for online or in-person purchases.

Any received funds can be transferred to the bank account of your choosing in one to three business days. Want your cash faster? Instant Transfer gives you access to your cash within just minutes for a fee of 1.5% (minimum of $0.25 and maximum of $15).

Venmo also offers a connected credit card, allowing users to pay for purchases and earn up to 3% cash back. You can even purchase and manage a cryptocurrency wallet through the Venmo app. And if you pay for goods and services with Venmo, your eligible purchase is protected if something goes wrong.

Venmo is free to download and use. There are fees for expedited withdrawals to a bank account, as well as a 3% fee if you send money to someone else using a connected credit card account (linked bank account payments have no fees). Businesses also pay a 1.9% fee plus $0.10 of any received payments over a dollar.

Zelle is a money transfer platform that connects with your U.S.-based bank or credit union accounts. With Zelle, you can instantly move money from one enrolled bank account to the next, whether you’re sending funds to a loved one or paying for something you bought on Facebook Marketplace.

Rather than these funds landing in a middleman platform and then needing to be withdrawn to another bank account, Zelle allows you to make transfers that instantly withdraw from one bank account and deposit into the next. In most cases, this means that the funds are available for use right away.

In order to use Zelle, both users must enroll through the Zelle mobile app or their bank’s online banking platform. A U.S. checking or savings account is required to enroll. There is no fee to use Zelle’s services, and it’s available through more than 1,700 financial institutions.

Zelle isn’t a payment platform, so you can’t use it to make online purchases through a merchant’s website. You also won’t be offered any purchase protection if you pay a seller directly, and there is no option for disputing a transfer once it’s been sent. Be sure to send Zelle payments only to individuals you know and/or trust.

Best alternative to apps to transfer money

Not sure if you’re comfortable with a mobile money transfer app? Here are two other options to consider.

Lili

If you run your own company, Lili can help you manage your business checking and send or receive funds via seamless ACH transfer. This fintech charges no hidden fees, has no overdraft charges, and sets no minimum balance or deposit requirements to open or maintain an account.

If you need to send money to others, you have the option of standard or express ACH through connected payment apps. When it comes to receiving money, your Lili account can accept ACH transfers as well as fee-free domestic wire transfers without limits. There are also various accounting features and software that make it easier to manage your business’ bookkeeping along the way.

Apple Cash

If you are an Apple user looking to send cash to other Apple users, Apple Cash is another option to consider. With Apple Cash, you can send, receive, or request money from others in the Apple family with no fees.

Transfers can be funded with a connected debit or credit card, bank account, prepaid card, or an existing Apple Cash balance. You can send money through iMessage or by tapping the Apple Cash option in your device’s mobile wallet. The recipient then needs to accept the money once their notification arrives, at which time the funds will transfer.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

How do money transfer apps work?

Money transfer apps work by sending a digital payment, ACH transfer, or domestic or international wire from a sender (who funds the transaction) to a recipient. Depending on the app, these funds might be automatically deposited into a bank account, readily spent using a connected debit card, available for cash withdrawal, or can be transferred to another linked bank account.

In some cases, funds can be sent by depositing cash at an in-person location, and can even be picked up in the form of cash by the recipient. If funds are sent from one eligible country to another, these apps will also facilitate currency conversion from sender to recipient.

How much do money transfer apps cost?

Many money transfer apps are available free of charge, especially if you are sending cash domestically or to friends and family. Others charge fees on the merchant side if you use the app to make a purchase for goods or services.

There may be fees involved if you opt to transfer money through an app using a credit card, need an expedited transaction, or if the cash needs to change currencies. These fees can vary from a dollar or two up to hundreds of dollars, depending on how much you want to send and how you plan to fund the transaction.

Are money transfer apps safe?

Most money transfer apps are financial technology companies (fintech) with highly encrypted platforms to keep your money and information as safe as possible. Most of these companies are licensed and regulated by agencies in the U.S. and abroad. Even still, it’s important to send money only to people you know and trust, as there are very few (if any) protections available once your funds have been sent.

How long does it take to transfer money with an app?

Many apps allow you to send funds to others instantly. Depending on the platform, these funds may need to be transferred out to a connected bank account before they can be spent, or they may be immediately available in the account of your choosing.

Other platforms, especially those facilitating international transfers, may take minutes or even days before the funds are available.

Cash App vs. PayPal—which is better?

Cash App is a great choice if you want to send money to others through an app without fees, while also having access to a connected debit card and even the option to pay in cryptocurrency. Paypal may be the better choice, however, if you’re buying goods or services and want purchase protection, or completing an online transaction.

Remitly vs. MoneyGram—which is better?

Both Remitly and MoneyGram offer international money transfers with currency conversion and multiple funding options. With both companies, fees are noted upfront and you can track your money transfer every step of the way. In many cases, MoneyGram money transfers may be available in as soon as a few minutes, while Remitly offers home delivery services for money transfers, simplifying the entire process for your recipient.
