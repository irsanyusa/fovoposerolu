How to Watch the American Idol Season 22 Finale (and Katy Perrys Farewell) Online Fre

Posted by Martina Birk on Saturday, June 8, 2024

Jack Blocker, Abi Carter, and Will Moseley compete one last time in tonight's episode, which will also mark Perry's final appearance as an Idol judge.

The latest winner of “American Idol” will be crowned tonight as the long-running singing competition show bids farewell to judge Katy Perry.

Want to watch the “American Idol” finale live? The episode airs tonight, Sunday, May 19, at 8 p.m. ET/5 p.m. PT on ABC. You can watch the “Idol” season finale on TV through your local ABC affiliate.

GET: DIRECTV STREAM 5-DAY FREE TRIAL

Don’t have cable or watch to stream “American Idol” online? Watch the show on a live TV streaming service like DirecTV Stream or Fubo TV. DirecTV Stream includes ABC as part of its channel offerings, and has a 5-day free trial that you can use to livestream the “American Idol” finale online free.

Fubo offers a 7-day free trial to let you watch “American Idol” on ABC free without cable. Fubo’s free trial includes DVR, so you can record the “Idol” finale to watch back on-demand later. Both DirecTV Stream and Fubo let you watch “American Idol” online through your phone, tablet, computer or smart TV.

Jack Blocker, Abi Carter, and Will Moseley are the three remaining finalists for the Season 22 crown. Each of them will have one last chance to sing for the judges and for viewer votes from home, before host Ryan Seacrest announces the 2024 “American Idol” winner.

Perry, meantime, will be making her final appearance as a judge on “Idol,” after announcing her departure earlier this year. The singer joined Luke Bryan and Lionel Richie as “American Idol” judges in 2018 when ABC revived the singing competition after 15 seasons on FOX. While Bryan and Richie are expected to return for Season 23 of “Idol,” the network has yet to announce who will be replacing Perry on the judging panel.

Tonight’s “American Idol” finale is expected to feature a tribute to Perry. Guest performers on the finale, meantime, include James Bay, Season 14 “Idol” winner Nick Fradiani, Cody Johnson, Bishop Briggs, Hootie & The Blowfish, Jason Mraz, Seal, Cece Winans, New Kids on the Block, and Wynonna Judd. Bon Jovi will also be performing and serving as guest mentor for the final three contestants.
